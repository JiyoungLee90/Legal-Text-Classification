The case originated in an application (no. 10655/07) against the Republic of Turkey lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a German national, Ms Sevgi Braun (“the applicant”), on 21 February 2007.
The applicant was represented by Mr Nedim Erkuş, a lawyer practising in Ankara. The Turkish Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent.
On 4 September 2009 the President of the Second Section decided to give notice of the application to the Government.
The German Government, who were invited to indicate whether they wished to exercise their right to intervene in the proceedings (Article 36 § 1 of the Convention), did not express their wish.
The applicant was born in 1944 and lives in .
The applicant used to work as secretary on contractual basis at the Education Department of the Turkish Representation in  attached to the Ministry of Education.
On 31 December 1996 the Ministry annulled the contract.
On 2 June 1997 the applicant lodged a compensation case with the  against the Ministry of Education for her labour claims.
On 29 December 1998 the labour court issued a decision of non-jurisdiction which was upheld by the Court of Cassation on 10 February 1999.
Upon the decision of non-jurisdiction, on 15 March 1999 the applicant brought compensation proceedings before the  for her labour claims, such as bonus, notice and severance pay.
On 21 January 2000 the court issued a decision of non-jurisdiction and transferred the case to the Supreme Administrative Court.
On 7 May 2002 the  quashed the decision of 21 January 2002 holding that the matter fell within the jurisdiction of it.
On 29 November 2002 the Ankara Administrative Court dismissed the case as introduced out of time. Without examining the merits of the case, it established that the sixty days time-limit to bring compensation action before the administrative court had expired on 5 December 1996 whereas the applicant lodged her case with the court on 2 June 1997.
In her appeal petition of 21 March 2003 the applicant challenged the judgment of the administrative court and requested a hearing in the appeal proceedings.
On 25 October 2005 the  upheld the judgment of 29 November 2002, finding it to have been in accordance with the law and the procedural rules. It decided that it was not necessary to hold a hearing.
On 28 June 2006 the Supreme Administrative Court dismissed a request by the applicant for rectification of the judgment.
On 22 August 2006 the decision was served on the applicant.
