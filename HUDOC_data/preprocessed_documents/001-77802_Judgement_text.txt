The case originated in an application (no. 15969/02) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Russian national, Mr Vladimir Grigoryevich Nikitin (“the applicant”), on 15 June 2001.
The Russian Government (“the Government”) were represented by Mr P. Laptev, the Representative of the  at the European Court of Human Rights.
On 2 September 2005 the Court decided to give notice of the application to the Government. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
The applicant was born in 1936 and lives in the town of Vorkuta in the Komi Republic of the .
On 8 August 1994 the applicant sued his employer, a mining company, for payment of royalties. The first hearing, fixed for 25 August 1994, was adjourned due to the applicant's absence. The following hearings of 9 December 1994 and 20 January 1995 were also postponed upon his requests.
On 22 February 1995 the  found for the applicant. However, that judgment was quashed on appeal on 30 March 1995 and re-examination was ordered. The applicant amended his claims.
On 11 March 1996 the  ordered an expert study. The proceedings were stayed until January 1997 when the  received the expert report.  In April 1997 another expert study was ordered. The applicant again amended his claims.
On 16 May 1997 the  dismissed the action. That judgment was quashed on appeal on 25 August 1997 and the case was remitted for a fresh examination.
A judge was assigned to the case in December 1997 and the first hearing was listed for 13 November 1998. However, that hearing was adjourned because the parties defaulted.  The following hearing of 18 October 1999 was postponed upon the parties' request for provision of additional evidence.
On 22 October 1999 the  ordered an expert examination. The proceedings were resumed on 15 March 2000 after the expert report had been submitted to the .
Two hearings, fixed between March and 17 November 2000, were adjourned because the defendant defaulted and the court wanted to call an expert.
On 17 November 2000 the  ordered an expert study. The proceedings were resumed a week later.
On 19 February 2001 the  found for the applicant. The judgment was quashed on appeal and the case was remitted for re-examination on 21 May 2001.
A judge was assigned to the case on 22 August 2001. In September and November 2001 the presiding judge inquired several expert organisations about a possibility to perform expert studies. A hearing was fixed for 17 January 2002. However, it was adjourned for provision of additional evidence by the defendant. The following hearing was fixed for 26 March 2002.
On 27 March 2002 the  ordered another expert examination. The applicant appealed against that decision, but on 20 May 2002 his appeal was dismissed.
In May 2002 the  sent the case-file to an expert bureau. A month later the experts asked the  for additional documents. The documents were submitted to the experts in August 2002. In October and November 2002 the experts inquired the  about the fees for their work.
In February 2003 the  asked the Judicial Department of the  to bear the costs of the expert examination. It appears that the expert fees were paid in July 2003.
In August and October 2003 the  inquired the experts about the progress in their work. On 1 April 2004 the experts informed the  that the expert report had been submitted to it on 19 September 2002.
In May 2004 the experts once again sent the report to the . The report contained certain procedural defects and the  asked for corrections. In July and September 2004 the  repeated its request. No response followed.
From 29 January to 28 April 2005 the applicant lived in .
On 11 February 2005 the  received the corrected expert report. Three days later the proceedings were resumed and a hearing was fixed for 10 March 2005. The parties were summoned. The summonses to the applicant were sent to all known addresses, including one in .
The hearing of 10 March 2005 was postponed because the applicant defaulted. The advice of receipt concerning the applicant's summonses returned from  with the note indicating that the applicant had refused to accept them. The following hearing was fixed for 4 April 2005. The  sent summonses to all known addresses of the applicant, including one in . It also unsuccessfully attempted to summon the applicant by phone.
On 4 April 2005 the  disallowed the applicant's action because he had failed to attend two hearings and had not notified of the reasons for his absence.
The applicant applied to the  for annulment of the decision of 4 April 2005. On 20 October 2005 the  dismissed the request. The court held that the applicant had been properly summoned. Moreover, there was evidence that he had refused to accept the summonses for the hearing of 10 March 2005.
