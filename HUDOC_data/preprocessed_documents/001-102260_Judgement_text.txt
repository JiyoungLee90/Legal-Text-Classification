The case originated in an application (no. 9548/06) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Turkish national, Mr Yusuf Kenan Arslantay (“the applicant”), on 21 February 2006. The applicant was represented by Mr A.L. Koçer, a lawyer practising in . The Turkish Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent.
On 13 May 2009 the President of the Second Section decided to give notice of the application to the Government. It was also decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility (Article 29 § 1).
The applicant was born in 1986 and lives in .
Subsequent to his expulsion from a military academy due to his health problems, the applicant brought a case before the  against the Ministry of Defence (“the Ministry”) for damages. The applicant argued that the alleged delay in the diagnosis of his medical condition had led to his unfair expulsion, which had inflicted pecuniary and non-pecuniary damages on him.
On 24 November 2004 the  held a hearing, where it heard the parties and the opinion of the principal public prosecutor, which had previously been submitted to the court in writing. On 21 September 2005, relying on the findings of medical experts, the  refused the applicant’s claim for damages. On 23 November 2005 it dismissed the applicant’s rectification request.
A description of the relevant domestic law can be found in the decision of Karayiğit v. Turkey ((dec.), no. 45874/05, 23 September 2008).
