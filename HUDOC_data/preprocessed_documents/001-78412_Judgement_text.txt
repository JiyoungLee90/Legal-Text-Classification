The case originated in an application (no. 42475/04) against  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Ukrainian national, Mrs Valentina Petrovna Kravchuk (“the applicant”), on 19 July 2004.
The applicant was represented before the Court by Mr V. Bychkovskiy. The Ukrainian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agents, Mrs V. Lutkovska and Mr Y. Zaytsev.
On 15 March 2005 the Court decided to communicate the application to the Government. Under the provisions of Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility.
The applicant was born in 1940 and lives in the town of Krasnyy Luch, Lugansk region, .
In 2003 the applicant instituted proceedings in the  against her former employer, the Knyagininska State Mining Company (Шахта «Княгинінська» ДХК «Донбасантрацит»), for salary arrears and compensation for moral damage. On 5 June 2003 the court awarded the applicant 3,300.02 Ukrainian hryvnias (“UAH”) (Рішення Краснолуцького міського суду Луганської області).
In July 2003 the Krasnolutskyy Town Bailiffs' Service (Відділ Державної виконавчої служби Краснолуцького міського управління юстиції) initiated the enforcement proceedings.
By letter of 2 June 2004, the Bailiffs' Service informed the applicant that the debtor's accounts had been frozen, that it was impossible to seize the debtor's property because the debtor was a State enterprise.
On 25 March 2005 the  initiated bankruptcy proceedings against the debtor enterprise.
The applicant received UAH 1,187.98; according to her, the rest of the debt remains unpaid.
The relevant domestic law is summarised in the judgment of Romashov v. Ukraine (no. 67534/01, §§ 16-18, 27 July 2004).
