The case originated in an application (no. 42857/02) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Slovenian national, Mr Davor Irgolič (“the applicant”), on 26 November 2002.
The applicant was represented by the Verstovšek lawyers. The Slovenian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mr L. Bembič, State Attorney-General.
The applicant alleged under Article 6 § 1 of the Convention that the length of the proceedings before the domestic courts to which he was a party was excessive. In substance, he also complained about the lack of an effective domestic remedy in respect of the excessive length of the proceedings (Article 13 of the Convention).
On 15 September 2004 the Court decided to communicate the complaints concerning the length of the proceedings and the lack of remedies in that respect to the Government. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility.
The applicant was born in 1984 and lives in Štore.
On 2 February 1997 the applicant was injured in a ski-resort K. The operator of the resort had taken out insurance with the insurance company ZM.
On 10 May 1999 the applicant instituted civil proceedings against the operator and ZM in the Celje District Court (Okrožno sodišče v Celju) seeking damages in the amount of 4,353,160 Slovenian tolars (approximately 18,140 euros) for the injuries sustained.
Between 8 June 2000 and 8 October 2004 the applicant lodged six preliminary written submissions.
Between 11 September 2000 and 15 November 2002 he made seven requests that a date be set for a hearing.
On 5 November 2001 the case judge was appointed to a  and the case was thus allocated to a new judge.
Of the three hearings held between 31 January 2001 and 12 January 2005 none was adjourned at the request of the applicant.
During the proceedings the court appointed a skiing expert.
At the last hearing the court decided to deliver a written judgment. The judgment, rejecting the applicant’s claim, was served on the applicant on 6 May 2005.
On 12 May 2005 the applicant appealed to the  (Višje sodišče v Celju).
The proceedings are still pending.
