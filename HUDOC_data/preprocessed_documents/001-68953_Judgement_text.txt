The case originated in an application (no. 35282/02) against  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Ukrainian national, Mr Viktor Nikolayevich Demchenko (“the applicant”), on 27 August 2002.
The Ukrainian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agents, Mrs V. Lutkovska and Mrs Z. Bortnovska.
On 30 September 2003 the Court decided to communicate the application to the Government. Under the provisions of Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility.
The applicant was born in 1953 and lives in the city of .
In 2001 the applicant instituted civil proceedings in the Kuybyshevskiy Local Court of Donetsk against his former employer, a State-owned mining company, in order to receive salary arrears.
On 23 January 2002 the court found for the applicant and awarded him UAH 5,089.99. This judgment was not appealed against and came into force on 24 February 2002. On 26 February the Kuybyshevsky Local Bailiffs’ Service instituted proceedings to enforce the above judgment.
By letter of 9 August 2002, the Bailiffs’ Service informed the applicant about the measures taken by the bailiffs to enforce the judgment and the limitations on enforcement proceedings due to the operation of the Law of Ukraine “on the Introduction of a Moratorium on the Forced Sale of Property”.
On 29 October 2003 the Bailiffs’ Service, following the restructuring of the debtor company, replaced the debtor by its legal successor, the Donetskvugillya State-owned Company. The enforcement proceedings were transferred accordingly to another Local Bailiffs’ Service for further actions in November 2003.
On 11 May 2004 the awarded amount was paid to the applicant in full and the enforcement proceedings were completed.
The relevant domestic law is summarised in the judgment of Romashov v.  (no. 67534/01, §§ 16-18, 27 July 2004).
