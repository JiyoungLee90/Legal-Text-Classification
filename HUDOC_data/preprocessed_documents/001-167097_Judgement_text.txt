The case originated in an application (no. 22727/08) against the Russian Federation lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Russian national, Mr Aleksey Nikolayevich Plotnikov (“the applicant”), on 5 April 2008.
The applicant was represented by Mr Y. Kruchinin, a lawyer practising in Cheboksary. The Russian Government (“the Government”) were represented by Mr G. Matyushkin, Representative of the Russian Federation to the European Court of Human Rights.
On 3 November 2011 the complaint concerning the length of the applicant’s pre-trial detention was communicated to the Government.
The applicant was born in 1960 and lived, prior to arrest and conviction, in Cheboksary.
On 27 January 2004 the applicant was arrested. He remained in custody pending investigation and trial.
On 7 October 2005 the Leninskiy District Court of Cheboksary convicted the applicant of fraud and sentenced him to six years and nine months’ imprisonment.
On 13 December 2005 the Supreme Court of the Chuvash Republic upheld his conviction on appeal. The court reduced the applicant’s sentence to five years and ten months.
On 22 June 2007 the Presidium of the Supreme Court of the Chuvash Republic quashed the judgments of 7 October and 13 December 2005 and remitted the case for a fresh examination. The applicant was remanded in custody.
On 11 July 2007 the Leninskiy District Court ordered the applicant to stay in prison pending trial.
