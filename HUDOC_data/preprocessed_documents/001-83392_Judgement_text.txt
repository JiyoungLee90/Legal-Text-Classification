The case originated in an application (no. 44885/04) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Hungarian national, Mr Huba Borody (“the applicant”), on 7 September 2004.
The applicant was represented by Mr Gy. Horváth, a lawyer practising in . The Hungarian Government (“the Government”) were represented by Mr L. Höltzl, Agent, Ministry of Justice and Law Enforcement.
On 11 January 2007 the Court decided to give notice of the application to the Government. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
The applicant was born in 1939 and lives in .
In a real estate dispute, on 2 January 1996 Mr B. brought an action against the applicant.
Between 23 April 1996 and 5 July 2001, the Budapest II/III District Court held altogether 14 hearings and obtained the opinion of an expert.
At the hearing of 9 October 2001 the respondent introduced his counter-claim, claiming the division of common property. Further hearings took place on 7 March and 11 June 2002.
From 4 February 2004 the case was stayed pending the parties' attempt to reach an agreement.
Further hearings were held on 21 June, 28 September and 3 December 2004. On the latter date the case was again stayed at the parties' request.
The parties requested the resumption of the proceedings on 6 June 2005, a stay on 18 July 2005, and the scheduling of a hearing on 5 January 2006. However, since they did not appear at the hearing on 9 March 2006, the case was again stayed. After the expiry of the statutory six-month time-limit, the case ceased to exist on 9 September 2006.
