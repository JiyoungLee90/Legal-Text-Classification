The case originated in an application (no. 12422/10) against the Republic of Croatia lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by two Serbian nationals, Mr Milan Aleksić and Ms Olga Aleksić (“the applicants”), on 8 January 2010.
The applicants were represented by Mr N. Mamula, a lawyer practising in Karlovac. The Croatian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Ms Š. Stažnik.
On 18 May 2011 the application was communicated to the Government.
The applicants were born in 1938 and 1944 respectively and live in .
On 15 November 2001 the applicants applied to the Karlovac Municipal Court (Općinski sud u Karlovcu) for enforcement of a civil court judgment awarding him damages adopted on 28 August 1991 by the Šabac Municipal Court in Serbia.
On 4 March 2005 the case was forwarded to the Zagreb Municipal Court (Općinski sud u Zagrebu).
The applicants’ motion for enforcement was declared inadmissible on 24 October 2005 on the ground that the Šabac Municipal Court judgment had never been recognised in Croatia.
This decision was upheld by the Zagreb County Court (Županijski sud u Zagrebu) on 3 January 2006 and served its decision on the applicants’ representative on 17 February 2006.
On 20 March 2006 the applicants lodged a constitutional complaint with the Constitutional Court (Ustavni sud Republike Hrvatske).
On 10 June 2009 the Constitutional Court dismissed the applicants’ constitutional complaint as ill-founded.
The decision of the Constitutional Court was served on the applicants’ representative on 10 July 2009.
