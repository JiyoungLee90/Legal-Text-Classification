The case originated in an application (no. 4578/03) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Hungarian national, Mr János Bencze (“the applicant”), on 2 December 2002.
The Hungarian Government (“the Government”) were represented by Mr L. Höltzl, Agent, Ministry of Justice and Law Enforcement.
On 29 June 2005 the Court decided to give notice of the application to the Government. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.

In May 1990 a company brought an action in damages against the applicant. On 1 November 1991 the Supreme Court appointed the  to hear the case. On 22 July 1992 the applicant brought a counter-claim.
After having held three hearings and having obtained the opinion of an expert, on 2 December 1996 the  dismissed all claims.
On 18 February 1998 the Supreme Court, sitting as a second-instance court, quashed the first-instance decision in its part concerning the applicant's counter-claim and remitted it to the .
On 21 May 1999 the  held a hearing and appointed an expert.
On 25 February 2000 the  ordered the plaintiff to pay the applicant 1,300,000 Hungarian forints plus accrued interest and dismissed the remainder of his claims.
On appeal, on 22 May 2002 the Supreme Court upheld the first-instance decision.
