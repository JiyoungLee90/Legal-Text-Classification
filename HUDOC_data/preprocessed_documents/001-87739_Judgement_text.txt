The case originated in an application (no. 9286/04) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Hungarian national, Mr János Sterbek (“the applicant”), on 20 January 2004.
The Hungarian Government (“the Government”) were represented by Mr L. Höltzl, Agent, Ministry of Justice and Law Enforcement.
On 12 October 2006 the Court decided to give notice of the application to the Government. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
The applicant was born in 1950 and lives in .
In 1992 the applicant brought an action in compensation against his former employer. On 12 April 1995 the  found for him. On 18 July 1995 enforcement proceedings were instituted. Since the respondent’s liquidation had been initiated on 14 December 1995, the applicant’s claims were channelled into those proceedings in April 1996.
On 13 October 1997 the applicant brought an official liability action against the , seeking compensation for the damage which the respondent court had allegedly caused by not having proceeded with the enforcement of the award due to the applicant (see paragraph 5 above) adequately or in good time. On 3 March 1998 the  was appointed to hear the case.
After two hearings, this procedure was suspended on 27 April 1999, pending the termination of the liquidation.
The liquidation was accomplished on 11 March 2002. The sum due to the applicant could not be recovered for want of assets.
The applicant requested that the official liability proceedings be resumed only on 10 June 2004; he had not been able to do so earlier because he had suffered a serious occupational accident.
After a hearing on 22 November 2004, on 9 March 2005 the  dismissed the applicant’s claims. He did not appeal.
