The case originated in an application (no. 1461/02) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Slovenian national, Mr Zoran Marinović (“the applicant”), on 4 January 2002.
The applicant was represented by the Verstovšek lawyers. The Slovenian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mr L. Bembič, State Attorney-General.
The applicant alleged under Article 6 § 1 of the Convention that the length of the proceedings before the domestic courts to which he was a party was excessive. In substance, he also complained about the lack of an effective domestic remedy in respect of the excessive length of the proceedings (Article 13 of the Convention).
On 11 June 2004 the Court decided to communicate the complaints concerning the length of the proceedings and the lack of remedies in that respect to the Government. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
The applicant was born in 1978 and lives in Velenje.
On 15 July 1996 the applicant was injured in an accident at work in a lignite mine. The applicant’s employer had taken out insurance with the insurance company ZT.
On 14 March 1997 the applicant instituted civil proceedings against his employer and ZT in the  (Okrajno sodišče v Celju) seeking damages in the amount of 1,000,000 tolars (approximately 4,100 euros) for the injuries sustained.
Between 6 March 1997 and 25 March 1999 the applicant made six requests that a date be set for a hearing.
Between 15 June 1998 and 28 November 2000 he lodged six preliminary written submissions and/or adduced evidence.
Of the five hearings held between 8 June 1998 and 6 December 2000 none was adjourned at the request of the applicant.
During the proceedings the court appointed a medical expert.
At the last hearing the court decided to deliver a written judgment. The judgment, upholding the applicant’s claim in part, was served on the applicant on 27 December 2000.
On 29 December 2000 the applicant appealed to the  (Višje sodišče v Celju).
On 17 October 2001 the court dismissed the applicant’s appeal.
The judgment was served on the applicant on 22 November 2001.
