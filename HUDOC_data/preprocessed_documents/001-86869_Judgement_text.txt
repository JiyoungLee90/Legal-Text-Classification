The case originated in an application (no. 7680/05) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Hungarian national, Ms Teréz Koronczai (“the applicant”), on 28 January 2005.
The Hungarian Government (“the Government”) were represented by Mr L. Höltzl, Agent, Ministry of Justice and Law Enforcement.
On 14 June 2007 the Court decided to give notice of the application to the Government. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
The applicant was born in 1953 and lives in Székesfehérvár.
In the context of her dismissal from employment with the State Railways, on 29 April 2003 the applicant brought an action before the  claiming outstanding wages. She requested legal aid. On 9 October 2003 the case was transferred to the . However, a legal-aid lawyer was appointed for the applicant only on 22 March 2004.
On 1 September 2004 the lawyer submitted the applicant’s recapitulated action.
On 5 and 12 May and 29 September 2005 hearings took place. On the latter date, a partial decision was adopted.
On 8 May 2006 the remainder of the applicant’s claims was partially accepted. On 25 September 2006 the applicant appealed.
On 11 July 2007 the  upheld the first instance decision.
