The case originated in an application (no. 10614/02) against  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Ukrainian national, Mr Ivan Adamovich Shiker (“the applicant”), on 11 February 2002.
The Ukrainian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mrs Z. Bortnovska.
On 9 September 2003 the Court decided to communicate the application to the Government. Under the provisions of Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility.
The applicant was born in 1952 and lives in the town of ,  region, .
On 24 April 1998 the  ordered the Belitskaya Mine, a State enterprise, to pay the applicant UAH 15,675.40 in compensation for an occupational disability and for moral damage. The applicant failed to inform the Court whether he appealed against this judgment or not.
In July 1998 the Dobropolye Town Bailiffs’ Service opened the enforcement proceedings.
Between 1998 and 2003 the judgment was enforced in instalments, the final payment being transferred on 10 November 2003.
On 12 November 2003 the Bailiffs’ Service terminated the enforcement proceedings in view of the full enforcement of the judgment given in the applicant’s favour.
The relevant domestic law is summarised in the judgment of Romashov v. Ukraine (no. 67534/01, §§ 16-18, 27 July 2004).
