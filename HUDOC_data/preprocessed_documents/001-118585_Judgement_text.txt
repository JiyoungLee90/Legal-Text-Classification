The case originated in an application (no. 42224/04) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Slovenian national, Ms Zdenka Podbelšek Bračič (“the applicant”), on 15 November 2004.
The Slovenian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent.
On 8 March 2012 the application was communicated to the Government.
The applicant was born in 1963 and lives in Kamnik.
On 28 November 1998 the applicant instituted civil proceedings concerning essentially a division of joint property before the Ljubljana District Court. On 14 December 1998 the applicant’s claim was referred to the . The proceedings were initially conducted before the  (First set of proceedings) but were later separated into two sets of proceedings, and in part referred to the Ljubljana District Court (second set of proceedings).
Between 1999 and 2001 the  held three hearings.
On 19 June 2002 the applicant lodged a request to refer her case to another court having subject matter jurisdiction. Due to the applicant’s request a hearing scheduled for 26 June 2002 was adjourned.
On 5 September 2002 the Supreme Court dismissed the applicant’s request.
After the applicant had amended her claim on 9 January 2003, the local court postponed the hearing scheduled for 14 January 2003 in order to allow the defendant to reply to the amended claim.
On 12 September 2003 the applicant lodged a motion for an interim order asking the court to order the defendant to vacate the disputed premises and to order an entry in the land register prohibiting the alienation and encumbrance of the property. On 20 October 2003 the  partially rejected the applicant’s motion. The applicant appealed.
On 18 February 2004 the Ljubljana Higher Court dismissed the applicant’s appeal.
On 6 June 2005 the  issued a decision to suspend the proceedings. On 14 September 2005 the  annulled the decision suspending the proceedings.
Between 2003 and 2005 the applicant lodged two supervisory appeals with the Ministry of Justice and on several occasions requested the  to schedule a hearing and decide the case.
At a hearing on 1 January 2006 the  decided to postpone the main hearing until 10 March 2006 in order to allow the defendant to consider the settlement proposal of the applicant.
After the parties to the procedure failed to reach a settlement, the , after holding a hearing on 10 March 2006, on that day dismissed the claim of the applicant. The applicant appealed.
On 21 March 2007 the applicant’s appeal was dismissed by the . Subsequently the applicant lodged an appeal on the points of law with the Supreme Court.
On 24 May 2007 the Supreme Court discontinued the proceedings due to the settlement reached in the second set of proceedings and the applicant’s withdrawal of the appeal.
The second set of proceedings relates to the part of the applicant’s initial claim that exceeded the competence of the local court and was referred to the Ljubljana District Court on 7 January 2004.
Five hearings were held between 6 July 2004 and 22 May 2007. On the latter date the parties reached a settlement agreement and the proceedings were discontinued on 28 May 2007.
For relevant domestic law see Tomažič v. Slovenia (no. 38350/02, 13 December 2007).
