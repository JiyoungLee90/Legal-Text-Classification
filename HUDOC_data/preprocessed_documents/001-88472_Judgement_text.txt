The case originated in an application (no. 35893/04) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Russian national, Mr Viktor Nikolayevich Levin (“the applicant”), on 10 June 2004.
The Russian Government (“the Government”) were represented by Mr P. Laptev and Ms V. Milinchuk, former Representatives of the  at the European Court of Human Rights.
On 23 October 2006 the Court decided to give notice of the application to the Government. It also decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility (Article 29 § 3).
The applicant was born in 1949 and lives in Gorodovikovsk, a town in Kalmykia.
As a victim of , the applicant was entitled to benefits. Considering himself underpaid, he sued the local welfare authority.
On 15 September 2003 the Gorodovikovskiy District Court of Kalmykia awarded the applicant arrears and fixed a new amount of periodic benefits. This judgment became binding on 4 December 2003 and was enforced on 18 November 2005.
Under section 9 of the Federal Law on Enforcement Proceedings of 21 July 1997, a bailiff must enforce a judgment within two months. Under section 242.2.6 of the Budget Code of 31 July 1998, the Ministry of Finance must enforce a judgment within three months.
