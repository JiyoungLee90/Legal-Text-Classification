The case originated in an application (no. 15259/02) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Turkish national, Mr Hasan Ertürk (“the applicant”), on 31 January 2002.
The applicant was represented by Mr M. Bektaş, a lawyer practising in . The Turkish Government (“the Government”) did not designate an Agent for the purpose of the proceedings before the Court.
On 21 January 2004 the Court decided to communicate the application. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
The applicant was born in 1959 and lives in .
On 21 November 1983 the applicant was arrested and taken into police custody on suspicion of membership of an illegal organisation, the DevYol (Devrimci Yol - the ).
On 30 December 1983 the  ordered the applicant’s detention on remand.
On 14 December 1988 the applicant was released pending trial.
On 19 July 1989 the  convicted the applicant of membership of an illegal organisation and sentenced him to seven years’ imprisonment.
On 28 December 1996 the Court of Cassation quashed the judgment of the  on the ground that the latter had misinterpreted the domestic law in respect of the offence in question.
Subsequent to the promulgation of Law no. 3953 on 27 December 1993, which abolished the jurisdiction of the Martial Law Courts, the  acquired jurisdiction in the applicant’s case.
On 6 May 1996 the  commenced the applicant’s trial.
On 28 May 2004 the applicant was convicted of attempting to undermine the constitutional order under Article 146 of the Criminal Code. The first-instance court however held that there was no need for the applicant’s imprisonment, taking into account his previous custody and detention on remand.
The proceedings are still pending.
