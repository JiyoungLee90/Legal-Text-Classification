The case originated in an application (no. 40322/02) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Russian national, Mr Anatoliy Aleksandrovich Petukhov (“the applicant”), on 2 October 2002.
The Russian Government (“the Government”) were represented by Mr P. Laptev, the former Representative of the  at the European Court of Human Rights.
On 27 February 2006 the Court decided to communicate the complaint concerning the non-enforcement of domestic judgments to the Government. Under the provisions of Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility.
The applicant was born in 1949 and lives in .
In August 1997 the applicant was mistreated by the police. He brought two civil actions against them.
On 29 March 2000 and 9 January 2001 the Oktyabrskiy District Court of Kirov held for the applicant and awarded him compensation. It appears that both judgments became binding ten days after their pronouncement. The judgment of 29 March 2000 was not enforced, because the Ministry of Finance had lost the writ of enforcement. The judgment of 9 January 2001 was enforced on 5 June 2006.
Under section 9 of the Federal Law on Enforcement Proceedings of 21 July 1997, a bailiff must enforce a judgment within two months. Under section 242.2.6 of the Budget Code of 31 July 1998, the Ministry of Finance must enforce a judgment within three months.
