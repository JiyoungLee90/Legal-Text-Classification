The case originated in five applications (nos. 77208/16, 77506/16, 79424/16, 12537/17, 26118/17) against the Russian Federation lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by five Russian nationals. Their details appear in Appendix I below.
The Russian Government (“the Government”) were represented by Mr M. Galperin, Representative of the Russian Federation to the European Court of Human Rights.
On 8 February 2018 notice of the complaints concerning alleged partiality of the tribunal were given to the Government and the remainder of the applications was declared inadmissible pursuant to Rule 54 § 3 of the Rules of Court.
The applicants were found liable for an administrative offence of drunk driving. Mr Sodomtsev (application no. 12537/12) was also found liable for an administrative offence of refusal to take a breath test. The court imposed on them a monetary fine in the amount of 30,000 Russian roubles and suspended temporarily their driving licenses. The details pertaining to each application are summed up in Appendix II below.
For a summary of the relevant domestic provisions and practice, see the case of Karelin v. Russia (no. 926/08, §§ 22-37, 20 September 2016).
