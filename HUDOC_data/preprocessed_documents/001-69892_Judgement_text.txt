The case originated in an application (no. 15366/03) against  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Ukrainian national, Mr Vladimir Ilyich Chernyayev (“the applicant”), on 16 April 2003.
The Ukrainian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agents, Mrs V. Lutkovska and Mrs Z. Bortnovska.
On 19 January 2004 the Court decided to communicate the application to the Government. Under the provisions of Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility.
The applicant was born in 1938 and lives in the town of ,  region, .
On 4 August 2000 the  awarded the applicant UAH 3,150.00 in salary arrears against his former employer – the Southern Machinery Construction factory (the majority share of which – 70% – was owned by the State). The judgment was not appealed and became final.
On 3 July 2001 the  awarded the applicant UAH 1,180.00 in compensation for moral damage against the same factory.
By letter of 14 February 2003, the Head of the Kherson Regional Department of Justice of  informed the applicant that the enforcement proceedings against the factory had been stayed due to the opening of bankruptcy proceedings against the debtor.
On 17 May 2004 the  awarded the applicant UAH 13,460.72 in compensation for the delay in payment of the salary arrears against the same debtor. The enforcement proceedings in respect of this judgment were initiated on 24 June 2004.
The judgments remain unenforced.
The relevant domestic law is summarised in the judgment of Romashov v. Ukraine (no. 67534/01, §§ 16-18, 27 July 2004).
