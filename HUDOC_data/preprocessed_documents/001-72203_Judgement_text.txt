The case originated in an application (no. 6028/02) against  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Ukrainian national, Mr Stanislav Nikolayevich Malinovskiy (“the applicant”), on 22 January 2002.
The Ukrainian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mrs Z. Bortnovska.
On 9 September 2003 the Court decided to communicate the application to the Government. Under the provisions of Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility.
The applicant was born in 1949 and lives in the town of ,  region, .
On 10 January 2000 the  ordered the Central Mine, a State enterprise, to pay the applicant UAH 1,074.52 in salary arrears. The applicant failed to inform the Court whether he appealed against this judgment or not.
In January 2000 the Dimitrov Town Bailiffs’ Service opened the enforcement proceedings.
On 22 September 2003 the judgment was enforced in full and the Bailiffs’ Service terminated the enforcement proceedings.
The relevant domestic law is summarised in the judgment of Romashov v. Ukraine (no. 67534/01, §§ 16-18, 27 July 2004).
