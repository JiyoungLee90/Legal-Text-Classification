The case originated in an application (no. 33706/05) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Russian national, Ms Lyudmila Alekseyevna Aleksentseva (“the applicant”), on 8 May 2004.
The applicant was represented by Mr V.I. Tarnarutskiy, a lawyer practising in . The Russian Government (“the Government”) were represented by Mr P. Laptev, Representative of the  at the European Court of Human Rights.
On 13 October 2005 the Court decided to give notice of the application to the Government. Under the provisions of Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility.
The applicant was born in 1963 and lives in .
She brought civil proceedings against her local authority requesting arrears of child benefits due to her.
On 15 March 2001 the Central District Court of Voronezh granted her claims and awarded her the arrears in the amount of 6,945.10 roubles (RUR). The judgment was not appealed against and became final on
On 23 May 2001 a writ of execution was issued.
On 20 December 2001 the writ was returned to the applicant by the bailiff's service on account of the lack of funds in the debtor's account.
The applicant forwarded the writ of execution to the debtor's bank and requested it to debit the sum due from the debtor's account, but the bank refused.
She then brought proceedings against the bank claiming damages for its refusal to enforce the judgment of 15 March 2001.
On 10 November 2003 the Central District Court of Voronezh dismissed by a final judgment the applicant's claims on the ground that the bank had not been at fault in refusing to enforce the judgment of 15 March 2001.
On 13 December 2005 the judgment of the Central District Court of Voronezh was fully executed.
