The case originated in an application (no. 50933/07) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by an Albanian national, Mr Stefanaq Cale (“the applicant”), on 5 November 2007.
The Albanian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Ms L. Mandia of the State Advocate’s Office.
On 11 July 2011 the application was communicated to the Government.
The applicant was born in 1927 and lives in Korça.
On 9 May 2003 the Korça District Court ordered the  to pay the applicant and his two siblings 2,801,327 Albanian leks (“ALL”) as compensation for the nationalisation of their house by the State in 1975. That decision became final on 23 June 2003, no appeal having been lodged against it.
On 24 September 2003 an enforcement writ was issued.
To date, the applicant has been paid only ALL 705,291.
The relevant domestic law and practice has been described in the cases of Qufaj Co. Sh.p.k. v. Albania (no. 54268/00, §§ 21-26, 18 November 2004) and Gjyli v. Albania (no. 32907/07, §§ 19-28, 29 September 2009).
