The case originated in an application (no. 21742/02) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Hungarian national, Mr Imre Miklós (“the applicant”), on 30 November 2000.
The applicant was represented by Mr Gy. Tímár, a lawyer practising in . The Hungarian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mr L. Höltzl, Deputy State-Secretary, Ministry of Justice.
On 30 June 2004 the Court decided to communicate the complaint concerning the length of the proceedings to the Government. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
The applicant was born in 1946 and lives in .
On 9 November 1994 criminal proceedings were instituted against the applicant, a bank manager, and his accomplices on suspicion of fraud. In the ensuing proceedings, the applicant was assisted by defence counsel of his choice.
On 25 September 1996 the Borsod-Abaúj-Zemplén County Public Prosecutor’s Office preferred a bill of indictment against the applicant and two other individuals charging them with fraud and other offences.
After the completion of the bill of indictment, between 1 April and 21 October 1997 the , sitting as a first-instance court, held nine hearings. Subsequently the proceedings were halted on account of the non-appearance of a co-accused for trial and an unsuccessful motion for bias filed by the latter. The hearings resumed on 5 January 1999. Between that date and 2 June 1999 the court held 29 hearings altogether.
On 2 June 1999 the  convicted the applicant of aggravated mismanagement and sentenced him to two years’ imprisonment. The  relied on the testimonies of numerous witnesses, the opinions of experts and documentary evidence.
On 28 September 2000 the Supreme Court upheld the applicant’s sentence.
On 20 March 2001 the review bench of the Supreme Court rejected the applicant’s petition for review.
