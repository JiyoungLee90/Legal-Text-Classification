The case originated in an application (no. 33617/04) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Russian national, Mr Nikolay Ivanovich Kholodenko (“the applicant”), on 30 July 2004.
The Russian Government (“the Government”) were represented first by Mr P. Laptev and subsequently by Mrs V. Milinchuk, the former Representatives of the  at the European Court of Human Rights.
On 29 August 2006 the Court decided to give notice of the application to the Government. It also decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility (Article 29 § 3).
The applicant was born in 1956 and lives in , a town in the Volgograd Region.
The applicant sued the Volgograd Regional Department of the Judiciary, the Ministry of Justice, and the Treasury for damages caused by a negligent enforcement of a civil judgment. On 27 February 2003 the Voroshilovskiy District Court of Volgograd awarded the applicant 661,593.17 Russian roubles (RUB). This judgment became binding on 23 July 2003 and was enforced on 25 April 2006.
Under section 9 of the Federal Law on Enforcement Proceedings of 21 July 1997, a bailiff must enforce a judgment within two months. Under section 242.2.6 of the Budget Code of 31 July 1998, the Ministry of Finance must enforce a judgment within three months.
