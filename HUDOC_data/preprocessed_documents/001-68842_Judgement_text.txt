The case originated in an application (no. 75788/01) against  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Ukrainian national, Mr Sergey Alekseyevich Piryanik (“the applicant”), on 17 May 2001.
The Ukrainian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agents, Ms Valeria Lutkovska, succeeded by Ms Zoryana Bortnovska.
The applicant’s complaints under Articles 6 § 1 of the Convention were communicated to the respondent Government on 9 May 2003. On the same date the Court decided that Article 29 § 3 of the Convention should be applied and the admissibility and merits of the complaint be considered together.
The applicant and the Government each filed observations on the admissibility and merits (Rule 54A).
The applicant is a Ukrainian national who was born in 1970 and currently resides in Privolye, the Lugansk Region.
The applicant is an employee of the Kapustin mine of the State-owned Lysychanskugol Company (hereafter “the mine”), which is a structural division of the Ministry of Fuel and Energy of Ukraine.
According to the Government, all of the property of the mine has been in a tax lien since 24 July 1998. Moreover, since August 1997 the mine experienced financial difficulties and therefore salaries were not paid regularly to its employees.
On 25 January 2001 the labour disputes commission of the mine (“the commission”) ordered it to pay the applicant UAH 4,037.60 in compensation for unpaid salary for the period of 1997-2000.
Following this decision, the applicant complained to the Lysychansk City Court (the “”), seeking to institute proceedings against the mine in order to recover his salary. On 2 February 2001 the  refused to consider the applicant’s complaints since the commission had already allowed his claims and awarded compensation in his favour.
On 12 February 2001 the Lysychansk Execution Service (the “bailiffs’ ”) initiated execution proceedings. On 5 March 2001 the bailiffs informed the applicant that the commission’s decision could not be executed due to the mine’s lack of funds. On 3 May 2001 the Lugansk Regional Department of Justice confirmed this.
On 29 May 2001 the Arbitration Court of Lugansk instituted bankruptcy proceedings against the mine on the basis of the application lodged by the Luganskoblenergo Company. On 17 July 2001 the  upheld the settlement reached by the parties in the case and discontinued the bankruptcy proceedings.
On 16 October 2001 the  instituted bankruptcy proceedings against the mine on the basis of the application lodged by the Gorlivka Engineering Plant. These proceedings were also discontinued, on 23 September 2002, after a settlement.
On 28 November 2003 the mine transferred UAH 4,037.60 to the applicant’s account in the Lysychansk Branch of the Savings Bank of .
On 8 December 2003 the bailiffs’ terminated the enforcement proceedings in the case in view of their completion.
On 12 June 2003 the commission awarded the applicant UAH 2,789.26 in compensation for salary arrears for the period of 2001-2003.
On 14 May 2004 the bailiffs’ terminated the ensuing enforcement proceedings in view of their completion on 12 June 2003.
