The case originated in an application (no. 22597/02) against  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Ukrainian national, Mr Vasiliy Ivanovich Antonovskiy (“the applicant”), on 26 May 2001.
The Ukrainian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mrs V. Lutkovska.
On 2 July 2003 the Court decided to communicate the application to the Government. Under the provisions of Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility.
The applicant was born in 1967 and lives in the town of , the  region.
On 1 June 1999 the  ordered the Progres State Mine to pay the applicant UAH 11,695.98 in occupational disability allowances and salary arrears.
On 28 June 1999 the Torez Town Bailiffs’ Service instituted enforcement proceedings in respect of that judgment.
By three decisions of 19 January 2001, the labour disputes commission of the mine awarded the applicant UAH 16,197.63 in arrears of salary and occupational disability allowances.
According to the Government, the judgment and the commission’s decisions were partly enforced in instalments: in November 1999, December 2000, June and September 2001 the applicant received a total of UAH 6,329.45, on an unspecified date he received a total of UAH 8,500.79, and in July and September 2003 he was paid UAH 2,984.74.
The parties did not specify the date on which the applicant was paid the remaining amount of the awards of the judgment and the commission’s decisions awards (UAH 10,078.63).
On 22 September 2003 the Bailiffs’ Service terminated the enforcement proceedings in view of the full enforcement of the judgment and the commission’s decisions given in the applicant’s favour.
The relevant domestic law is summarised in the judgment of Romashov v. Ukraine (no. 67534/01, §§ 16-18, 27 July 2004).
