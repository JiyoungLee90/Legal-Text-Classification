The case originated in an application (no. 49905/06) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Hungarian national, Mr Bálint Csorba (“the applicant”), on 5 December 2006.
The applicant was represented by Mr K. Pencz, a lawyer practising in Kecskemét. The Hungarian Government (“the Government”) were represented by Mr L. Höltzl, Agent, Ministry of Public Administration and Justice.
On 9 November 2009 the President of the Second Section decided to give notice of the application to the Government. In accordance with Protocol No. 14, the application was allocated to a Committee of three Judges.
The applicant was born in 1946 and lives in Kecskemét.
In February 1992 the applicant brought an action against a limited liability company before the , requesting the court to order the respondent to pay him fee for using his real estate.
The  suspended the proceedings on three occasions for several years, pending the outcome of separate legal disputes related to the ownership of the real estate. The applicant’s requests to have the proceedings resumed were dismissed.
On 22 June 2006 the  delivered judgment, finding partly for the applicant. In the absence of appeals, the judgment became final on 17 July 2006.
