The case originated in an application (no. 17074/09) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by three Turkish nationals, Ms Fatma Erkızan, Mr Hakkı Bolkan Erkızan and Mr Haydar Burak Erkızan (“the applicants”), on 6 March 2009.
The applicants were represented by Ms N. Şimşek, a lawyer practising in İzmir. The Turkish Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent.
On 2 November 2010 the application was communicated to the Government.
The Government objected to the examination of the application by a Committee. After having considered the Government’s objection, the Court rejects it.
The applicants were born in 1962, 1989 and 1990 respectively and live in .
The applicants are the wife and sons of Ahmet Celil Erkızan who died in a ship wreck on 2 May 1994.
On 11 January 1995 the applicants brought a civil case before the Milas Civil Court of General Jurisdiction in request of pecuniary and non pecuniary damages.
On 3 November 2003 the  partially awarded their requests for damages.
On 1 July 2004 the Court of Cassation quashed the judgment based on the fact that the case was within the jurisdiction of labour courts.
Upon that decision the  began to hear the case.
On 21 February 2006 the Court of Cassation quashed the decision of the  which admitted the case of the applicants again.
On 16 July 2007 the  following the quashing decision of the Court of Cassation admitted the case partially.
On 8 July 2008 the Court of Cassation upheld the decision.
On 10 September 2008 the final decision was served to the applicants.
