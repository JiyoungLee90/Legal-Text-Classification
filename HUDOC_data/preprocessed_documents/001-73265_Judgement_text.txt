The case originated in an application (no. 25055/02) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by an Austrian national, Ms Doris Pfeiffer (“the applicant”) on 24 June 2002.
The applicant was represented by the Verstovšek lawyers. The Slovenian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mr L. Bembič, State Attorney-General.
The applicant alleged under Article 6 § 1 of the Convention that the length of the proceedings before the domestic courts to which she was a party was excessive. In substance, she also complained about the lack of an effective domestic remedy in respect of the excessive length of the proceedings (Article 13 of the Convention).
On 7 September 2004 the Court decided to communicate the complaints concerning the length of the proceedings and the lack of remedies in that respect to the respondent Government. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
In accordance with Article 36 § 1 of the Convention and Rule 44 of the Rules of Court, the Registrar informed the Austrian Government of their right to submit written comments. They did not indicate that they wished to exercise their right.
The applicant was born in 2002 and lives in Wien ().
On 1 June 1997 the applicant was bitten by a dog.
On 5 December 1997 the applicant instituted civil proceedings against the owner of the dog in the Ljubljana Local Court (Okrajno sodišče v Ljubljani) seeking damages in the amount of 708,363,50 tolars (approximately 2,950 euros) for the injuries sustained.
On 6 January and 18 March 1998 the applicant lodged preliminary written submissions and/or adduced evidence.
On 17 December 1998 the case was taken over by a new judge.
Between 8 January 1998 and 16 July 2002 the court made seven attempts to serve summons on the defendant but all were unsuccessful.
Between 16 October 1998 and 9 September 2002 she made six requests that a date be set for a hearing.
On 9 September and 8 October 2002 the applicant lodged preliminary written submissions.
On 12 December 2002 the court held a hearing in absence of the defendant although the summons had been served on him. The court decided to deliver a written judgment.
At the last hearing the court decided to deliver a written judgment.
The judgment, upholding the applicant’s claim, was served on the applicant on 16 May 2003.
