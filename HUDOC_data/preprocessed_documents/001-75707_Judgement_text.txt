The case originated in an application (no. 67255/01) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by Mr Göktekin Dolgun (“the applicant”) on 15 November 2000.
The applicant was represented by Mr Mahmut Akdoğan, a lawyer practising in . The Turkish Government (“the Government”) did not designate an Agent for the purposes of the proceedings before the Court.
On 15 September 2004 the Court decided to communicate the application to the Government. Under the provisions of Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility.
The applicant was born in 1934 and lives in .
On 20 July 1993 the General Directorate of National Roads and Highways expropriated two plots of land belonging to the applicant in  in order to build a motorway. A committee of experts assessed the value of the plot of land and the relevant amount was paid to the applicant when the expropriation took place.
Following the applicant’s request for increased compensation, on 16 June 1994 the  awarded him additional compensation in the amount of 343,826,000 Turkish liras (TRL), plus interest at the statutory rate, applicable at the date of the court’s decision, running from 20 July 1993, the date of transfer of the title deeds.
On 1 June 1998 the Court of Cassation upheld the judgment.
On 17 May 2000 the General Directorate of National Roads and Highways paid the applicant TRL 1,336,460,000, interest included.
The relevant domestic law and practice are set out in the Akkuş v. Turkey judgment of 9 July 1997 (Reports of Judgments and Decision 1997-IV).
