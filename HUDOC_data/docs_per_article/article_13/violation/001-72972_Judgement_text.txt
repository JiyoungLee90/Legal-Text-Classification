The case originated in an application (no. 75687/01) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Slovenian national, Mr Vlado Rojc (“the applicant”), on 9 November 2000.
The applicant was represented by the Verstovšek lawyers. The Slovenian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mr L. Bembič, State Attorney-General.
The applicant alleged under Article 6 § 1 of the Convention that the length of the proceedings before the domestic courts to which he was a party was excessive. In substance, he also complained about the lack of an effective domestic remedy in respect of the excessive length of the proceedings (Article 13 of the Convention).
On 8 July 2003 the Court decided to communicate the complaints concerning the length of the proceedings and the lack of remedies in that respect to the Government. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
The applicant was born in 1938 and lives in Celje.
On 24 August 1989 the applicant was injured in a car accident.
On 14 October 1991 the applicant instituted civil proceedings against two individuals in the , Celje Unit (Temeljno sodišče v Celju, Enta Celje) seeking damages in the amount of 1,082,037 tolars (approximately 4,500 euros) for the injuries sustained.
On 13 December 1993 and 29 March 1994 the applicant requested that a date be set for a hearing.
Of the five hearings held between 27 January 1992 and 27 June 1994 none was adjourned at the request of the applicant. During this time the court appointed a financial expert and a medical expert. The court also sought an additional opinion from the appointed financial expert.
On 1 January 1995 the  (Okrajno sodišče v Celju) gained the jurisdiction in the present case due to the reform of the Slovenian judicial system.
Between 3 November 1995 and 17 April 2000 the applicant made ten requests that a date be set for a hearing.
On 30 June 1999 and 12 June 2000 the court held hearings. At the latter hearing the parties reached a court settlement.
