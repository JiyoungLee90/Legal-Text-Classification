The case originated in an application (no. 75809/01) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Slovenian national, Mr Peter Bastič (“the applicant”), on 21 July 2001.
The applicant was represented by the Verstovšek lawyers. The Slovenian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mr L. Bembič, State Attorney-General.
The applicant alleged under Article 6 § 1 of the Convention that the length of the proceedings before the domestic courts to which he was a party was excessive. In substance, he also complained about the lack of an effective domestic remedy in respect of the excessive length of the proceedings (Article 13 of the Convention).
On 16 September 2003 the Court decided to communicate the complaints concerning the length of the proceedings and the lack of remedies in that respect to the Government. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
The applicant was born in 1973 and lives in Laško.
On 30 May 1995 the applicant was injured in a car accident. The perpetrator of the accident had taken out insurance with the insurance company ZM.
On 14 April 1997 the applicant instituted civil proceedings against ZM in the Celje District Court (Okrožno sodišče v Celju) seeking damages in the amount of 1,550,000 tolars (approximately 6,460 euros) for the injuries sustained.
Between 28 August 1998 and 8 March 2001 the applicant lodged eight preliminary written submissions and/or adduced evidence.
Between 10 November 1998 and 10 April 2001 he made five requests that a date be set for a hearing.
Of the four hearings held between 18 May 1999 and 12 June 2001, none was adjourned at the request of the applicant.
During the proceedings, the court appointed a medical expert. The court also sought an additional opinion from the appointed expert.
At the last hearing the court decided to deliver a written judgment. The judgment, upholding the applicant’s claim in part, was served on the applicant on 2 July 2001.
On 16 July 2001 the applicant appealed to the  (Višje sodišče v Celju).
On 9 October 2002 the court allowed the applicant’s appeal in part and increased the damages awarded.
The judgment was served on the applicant on 19 November 2002.
