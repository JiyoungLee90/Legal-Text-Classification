The case originated in an application (no. 38933/02) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Slovenian national, Mrs Marjana Šimek Hudomalj (“the applicant”), on 17 October 2002.
The applicant was represented by the Verstovšek lawyers. The Slovenian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mr L. Bembič, State Attorney-General.
The applicant alleged under Article 6 § 1 of the Convention that the length of the proceedings before the domestic courts to which she was a party was excessive. In substance, she also complained about the lack of an effective domestic remedy in respect of the excessive length of the proceedings (Article 13 of the Convention).
On 15 September 2004 the Court decided to communicate the complaints concerning the length of the proceedings and the lack of remedies in that respect to the Government. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility.
The applicant was born in 1960 and lives in Škofja vas.
On 21 June 1998 the applicant was injured in a car accident. The perpetrator of the accident had taken out insurance with the insurance company ZM.
On 7 December 1998 the applicant instituted civil proceedings against ZM in the Celje District Court (Okrožno sodišče v Celju) seeking damages in the amount of 5,758,030 Slovenian tolars (approximately 24,000 euros) for the injuries sustained.
Between 19 April 1999 and 6 September 2002 the applicant lodged ten preliminary written submissions.
On 9 March 2000 she made a request that a date be set for a hearing.
Of the three hearings held between 7 November 2000 and 15 October 2002, none was adjourned at the request of the applicant.
During the proceedings, the court appointed three medical experts.
At the last hearing, the court decided to deliver a written judgment. The judgment, upholding the applicant’s claim in part, was served on the applicant on 26 November 2002,
On 6 December 2002 the applicant appealed to the  (Višje sodišče v Celju) and also requested the correction of the first-instance court’s judgment.
On 13 December 2002 the first-instance court corrected its judgement.
On 25 March 2004 the court allowed the applicant’s appeal in part and increased the damages awarded.
The judgment was served on the applicant on 3 May 2004.
