The case originated in an application (no. 42274/02) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Slovenian national, Mrs Andreja Bizjak Jagodič (“the applicant”), on 19 November 2002.
The applicant was represented by the Verstovšek lawyers. The Slovenian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mr L. Bembič, State Attorney-General.
The applicant alleged under Article 6 § 1 of the Convention that the length of the proceedings before the domestic courts to which she was a party was excessive. In substance, she also complained about the lack of an effective domestic remedy in respect of the excessive length of the proceedings (Article 13 of the Convention).
On 15 September 2004 the Court decided to communicate the complaints concerning the length of the proceedings and the lack of remedies in that respect to the Government. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility.
The applicant was born in 1975 and lives in Vodice.
On 14 November 1998 the applicant was injured in a car accident. The perpetrator of the accident had taken out insurance with the insurance company ZM.
On 10 May 1999 the applicant instituted civil proceedings against ZM in the Celje District Court (Okrožno sodišče v Celju) seeking damages in the amount of 2,564,040 Slovenian tolars (approximately 10,690 euros) for the injuries sustained.
Between 21 July 1999 and 14 November 2002 the applicant lodged three written submission. She also partly withdrew her claim.
Between 12 January 2001 and 18 March 2002 she made three requests that a date be set for a hearing.
Of the two hearings held on 13 May 2002 and 14 November 2002, neither was adjourned at the request of the applicant.
During the proceedings, the court appointed a medical expert.
At the last hearing, the court decided to deliver a written judgment. The judgment, upholding the applicant’s claim in part, was served on the applicant on 3 March 2003.
On 13 March 2003 the applicant appealed to the  (Višje sodišče v Celju).
On 28 October 2004 the court allowed the applicant’s appeal in part.
The judgment was served on the applicant on 22 November 2004.
