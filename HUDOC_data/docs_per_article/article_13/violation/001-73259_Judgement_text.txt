The case originated in an application (no. 23571/02) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Slovenian national, Slavka Blatešič (“the applicant”), on 18 June 2002.
The applicant was represented by the Verstovšek lawyers. The Slovenian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mr L. Bembič, State Attorney-General.
The applicant alleged under Article 6 § 1 of the Convention that the length of the proceedings before the domestic courts to which she was a party was excessive. In substance, she also complained about the lack of an effective domestic remedy in respect of the excessive length of the proceedings (Article 13 of the Convention).
On 7 September 2004 the Court decided to communicate the complaints concerning the length of the proceedings and the lack of remedies in that respect to the Government. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
The applicant was born in 1953 and lives in Velenje.
On 6 October 1995 the applicant was injured in an accident at work. The applicant’s employer had taken out insurance with the insurance company A.
On 28 October 1997 the applicant instituted civil proceedings against A in the Celje District Court (Okrožno sodišče v Celju) seeking damages in the amount of 6,445,383 tolars (approximately 26,900 euros) for the injuries sustained.
Between 7 January 1998 and 21 December 2001 the applicant lodged eleven preliminary written submissions and/or adduced evidence.
During this time she made six requests that a date be set for a hearing.
Of the four hearings held between 12 February 1999 and 23 January 2002 none was adjourned at the request of the applicant.
On 5 November 2001 the presiding judge was appointed to the  (Višje sodišče v Celju) and the case was transferred to a new first-instance court judge.
During the proceedings the court appointed two medical experts and a work-safety expert.
At the last hearing the court decided to deliver a written judgment. The judgment, upholding the applicant’s claim in part, was served on the applicant on 21 May 2002.
On 5 June 2002 the applicant appealed to the . A cross-appealed.
The proceedings are still pending.
