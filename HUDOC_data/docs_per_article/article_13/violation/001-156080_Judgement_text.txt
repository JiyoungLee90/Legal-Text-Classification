The case originated in an application (no. 48322/12) against Hungary lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Hungarian national, Mr György Gazsó (“the applicant”), on 24 July 2012.
The applicant was represented by Mr D. Karsai, a lawyer practising in Budapest. The Hungarian Government (“the Government”) were represented by Mr Z. Tallódi, Agent, Ministry of Justice.
The applicant alleged that litigation in his labour dispute had lasted an unreasonably long time and there was no effective remedy available to him in this regard. He relied on Articles 6 and 13 of the Convention.
On 13 November 2014 the application was communicated to the Government. At the same time, the Court proposed the pilot judgment procedure (Rule 61 of the Rules of Court) to the parties. The parties submitted comments in that respect.
The applicant was born in 1963 and lives in Telki.
Between 25 February 2002 and 18 May 2005, litigation was in progress between the applicant and his former employer. The latter was eventually obliged to re-employ the applicant.

On 18 November 2008 the Budapest Labour Court dismissed the applicant’s action.
On appeal, on 9 July 2010 the Budapest Regional Court reversed this decision and found for the applicant.
The respondent pursued a petition for review before the Kúria. On 29 February 2012 this court reversed the appeal judgment and found for the respondent.
