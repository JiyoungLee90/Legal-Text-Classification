The case originated in applications against Romania lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) on the various dates indicated in the appended table.
The applications were communicated to the Romanian Government (“the Government”).
In application no. 40837/15, by letter dated 8 February 2017, the applicant notified the Court that he had changed his name, the new one being Ciprian-Florin Kalo. The Court advised the parties on 10 March 2017 that it would continue processing the application under the case name of Varga v. Romania. This corresponded to the applicant’s name as referred to in his application lodged with the Court.
The list of applicants and the relevant details of the applications are set out in the appended table.
The applicants complained of the inadequate conditions of their detention. Some applicants also raised other complaints under Article 3.
