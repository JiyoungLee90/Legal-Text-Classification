The case originated in an application (no. 54898/11) against the Republic of Turkey lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Turkish national, Mr İbrahim Kan (“the applicant”), on 20 July 2011.
The applicant was represented by Ms N. Karaman, a lawyer practising in Istanbul. The Turkish Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent.
On 10 January 2012 the application was communicated to the Government.
The applicant was born in 1974 and lives in Bolu. At the material time he was a military official.
On 18 February 2011 and 24 February 2011 the applicant’s superiors imposed on him an administrative restriction for 8 days and room confinement for 5 days as disciplinary penalties. The penalties were enforced between 4 and 9 April 2011 (5 days), 11 and 14 April 2011 (3 days), 29 April and 4 May 2011 (5 days).
The description of the relevant domestic law regarding administrative restrictions may be found in Gül v. Turkey (dec.) no. 74161/11, §§ 8-9, 10 July 2012, and regarding room confinements in Tengilimoğlu and Others v. Turkey, nos. 26938/08, 41039/09, 66328/09 and 66451/09, §§ 10-12, 5 June 2012.
