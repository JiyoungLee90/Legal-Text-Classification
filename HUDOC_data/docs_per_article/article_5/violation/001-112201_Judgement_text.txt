The case originated in an application (no. 25416/07) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Slovak national, Mr Róbert Winkler (“the applicant”), on 6 June 2007.
The Government of the  (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Ms M. Pirošíková.
The applicant alleged that he had been unlawfully deprived of his liberty and that the  had failed to provide appropriate redress to him.
On 26 August 2010 the application was communicated to the Government. It was also decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time (Article 29 § 1).
The applicant was born in 1977 and lives in Žilina.
On 26 November 2004 the Považská Bystrica District Court remanded the applicant in custody in connection with criminal proceedings in which he was accused of several offences.
On 20 May 2005 a public prosecutor indicted the applicant before the Považská Bystrica District Court.
On 10 August 2005 the applicant requested that he be released from detention. He argued that his detention under the decision of 26 November 2004 had expired on 25 May 2005 and that no decision had been taken to extend it.
The District Court dismissed the applicant’s request on 23 August 2005. On 12 September 2005 the  dismissed the applicant’s complaint against that decision.
On 14 November 2005 the applicant lodged a constitutional complaint alleging a breach of Article 5 § 1 of the Convention.
On 15 November 2005 the District Court convicted the applicant of two offences and sentenced him to two years’ imprisonment. The judgment became final on the same day.
On 6 December 2006 the  found that the , by its decision of 12 September 2005, had breached the applicant’s rights under Article 5 § 1 of the Convention. In particular, his detention had been unlawful between 26 May 2005 and 15 November 2005, as the District Court had not delivered a decision extending the applicant’s detention.
The  quashed the ’s decision, awarded the equivalent of 1,970 euros (at that time) in compensation to the applicant and ordered the  to reimburse the costs of the applicant’s legal representation in the constitutional proceedings.
