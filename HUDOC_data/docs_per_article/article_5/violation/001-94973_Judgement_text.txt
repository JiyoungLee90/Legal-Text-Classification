The case originated in an application (no. 6194/04) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Turkish national, Mr Ezgin Engin (“the applicant”), on 5 January 2004. The applicant was represented by Mr M. A. Kırdök and Mrs M. Kırdök, lawyers practising in Istanbul. The Turkish Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent.
On 10 September 2008 the President of the Second Section decided to give notice of the application to the Government. It was also decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility (Article 29 § 3).
The applicant was born in 1975 and is currently detained in the Kocaeli F Type Prison pending the criminal proceedings against him.
On 7 May 1997 the applicant was arrested and taken into custody on suspicion of being involved in the activities of an illegal organisation known as the DHKP-C (the Revolutionary People's Liberation Party).
On 10 May 1997 he was brought before a single judge at the  who ordered his pre-trial detention.
On 20 May 1997 the public prosecutor at the Istanbul State Security Court filed a bill of indictment against the applicant and another person, charging them with attempting to undermine the constitutional order under Article 146 § 1 of the former Criminal Code.
On 27 September 2001 the  convicted the applicant as charged and sentenced him to death.
On 14 May 2002 the Court of Cassation quashed the judgment of the first-instance court. The case was remitted to the  for further examination.
On 25 December 2003 the  convicted the applicant for a second time on the same charge and sentenced him to life imprisonment.
On 5 July 2004 the Court of Cassation once again quashed the judgment of the .
By Law no. 5190 of 16 June 2004, published in the Official Gazette on 30 June 2004, State Security Courts were abolished. The case against the applicant was subsequently resumed before the .
According to the information in the case file, the case is still pending before the .
Throughout the proceedings, the first-instance courts examined the applicant's continued detention at the end of every hearing, either on their own motion or upon the applicant's request. Each time the courts ordered his continued detention having regard to the state of evidence and the content of the file.
