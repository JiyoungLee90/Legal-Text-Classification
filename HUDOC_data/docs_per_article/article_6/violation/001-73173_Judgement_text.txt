The case originated in an application (no. 30081/02) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Hungarian national, Mr László Kalló (“the applicant”), on 26 June 2002.
The Hungarian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mr L. Höltzl, Deputy State-Secretary, Ministry of Justice.
On 29 June 2005 the Court decided to communicate the application. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
On 16 November 2005 eleven individuals (see Annex), all secondary relatives of the applicant (siblings, etc.), informed the Registry that the applicant had been found dead on 15 March 2004, however, the date of his death could not be determined more precisely. They stated that they were the applicant’s statutory heirs, in support of which they submitted an order of 11 July 2005 issued by the Kazincbarcika Public Notary on the transfer of the estate, and that they wished to pursue the application.
The applicant was born in 1941 and resided in Kazincbarcika. He died in March 2004.
On 2 September 1994 the applicant brought an action in the  against his former employer, a joint-stock company. He requested the court to hold that his employment contract had been terminated unlawfully, that he should be reinstated and that the agreement with the employer company to buy his shares from him be annulled.
After a hearing on 30 November 1994, on 15 February 1995 the  discontinued the proceedings partly because the applicant’s action had been submitted outside the statutory time-limit, and partly because it had no competence in the case.
On 11 May 1995 the  quashed part of the first-instance decision and appointed the Kazincbarcika District Court to deal with the applicant’s civil law claim.
On 16 February 1996 the Supreme Court accepted the applicant’s petition for review and remitted the labour law aspects of the case to the .
Meanwhile, on 25 April 1995 the applicant instituted proceedings, seeking the payment of damages, additional wages and other benefits. After a hearing on 6 December 1995, on 28 March 1996 the  ordered that this action be joined to the original proceedings which were to be resumed pursuant to the Supreme Court’s decision.
After hearings on 17 May and 7 June 1996, on 3 July 1996 the  gave a partial judgment. It stated that the company had terminated the applicant’s employment unlawfully and ordered it to reinstate him.
On appeal, on 28 November 1996 the  quashed the first-instance partial judgment and dismissed the applicant’s claim concerning the termination of his employment.
In the proceedings concerning the remainder of the case, on 8 April, 4 September and 2 November 1997, the  held hearings. On 16 December 1997 it accepted part of the applicant’s claims for additional wages and outstanding benefits, but dismissed the remainder.
On appeal, on 2 April 1998 the  considered the first-instance judgment to be a partial decision and confirmed its contents. It held that the  had failed to examine some of the applicant’s claims, and that in this respect the proceedings were to be continued.
In the resumed first-instance proceedings, on 15 June 1998 the  transferred part of the applicant’s claims to the competent Kazincbarcika District Court. After a hearing on 13 January 1999, on 3 February 1999 it dismissed the applicant’s action.
On 14 December 2000 the  dismissed the applicant’s appeal.
On 18 April 2002 the Supreme Court dismissed the applicant’s petition for review.
