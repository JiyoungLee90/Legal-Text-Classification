The case originated in an application (no. 9719/02) against  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by two Ukrainian nationals, Mr Yuriy Stepanovych Pomazanyy and Mr Vitaliy Leonidovych Shevchenko (“the applicants”), on 6 February 2002.
The Ukrainian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agents, Ms Zoryana Bortnovska, succeeded by Ms Valeria Lutkovska.
On 2 July 2003 the Court decided to communicate the complaint concerning the non-enforcement of the final judgments given in the applicants’ favour with regard to an alleged infringement of Article 6 § 1 of the Convention to the Government. Under the provisions of Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility.
The applicants were born in 1938 and 1951, respectively, and currently reside in Kyiv. They are former technical engineers of the State-owned company JSC “Air Company Ukrainian National Airlines” (the “UNA”).
On 6 May 1998 and 14 December 2001 the Zaliznychny District Court of Kyiv (the “”) ordered the UNA to pay the first applicant UAH 10,315.85 in compensation for salary arrears.
On 29 April and 14 September 1998, 25 November 1999 and 14 December 2002, the  ordered the UNA to pay the second applicant a total sum of UAH 7,231.02 in compensation for similar arrears.
On 25 November 1998 the judge of the  declared the UNA bankrupt. This judgment was quashed on 4 March 1999 by the First Deputy President of the Kyiv Court of Arbitration. The bankruptcy proceedings were suspended until privatisation of the debtor.
On 12 January 2000 the Zaliznychny Bailiffs’ Service (the “ZBS”) attached property belonging to the UNA. On 7 February 2000 the  suspended the sale of assets of the UNA in view of a complaint brought by the UNA against the ZBS.
On 18 January 2000 the Cabinet of Ministers ordered the privatisation of the UNA.
On 7 February 2000 the  rejected the UNA’s request to quash the ZBS’s decision to attach its property. On 5 April 2000 the Kyiv City Court quashed this judgment and remitted the case for a fresh examination to the .
On 21 August 2000 the enforcement proceedings were resumed by the ZBS in view of the judgment of 7 February 2000. However, they were again suspended due to the bankruptcy proceedings reinitiated against the UNA.
On 24 October 2000 the  rejected the UNA’s complaints against the actions of the ZBS. The enforcement proceedings were resumed.
On 12 December 2000 the enforcement proceedings were again suspended by a judge of the , in view of the UNA’s complaints against the ZBS.
On 11 April 2001 the ZBS informed the first applicant of a payment of UAH 210 made to him. The second applicant received the same sum.
On 6 September 2001 the UNA informed the applicants that the outstanding debt was UAH 8,570.71 (for the first applicant) and UAH 2,692.47 (for the second applicant).
On 22 August 2002 the  resumed examination of the UNA’s bankruptcy and ordered its creditors to hold a meeting. On 9 October 2002 the  approved the creditors’ claims register and suspended the bankruptcy proceedings.
On 29 September 2003 the Government informed the Court that each of the applicants had received UAH 540 in enforcement of the judgments given in their favour.
On 11 August 2005 the Kyiv City State Administration discussed a possibility of reducing the debt of the UNA and the possible proposals to be put in this respect to the State Property Fund of Ukraine (the total amount of debt was UAH 354,200).
On 5 September 2005 the Cabinet of Ministers approved the UNA privatisation scheme.
Since the enforcement proceedings were instituted the applicants have received part of the salary debts owed to them in small instalments. However the judgments given in their favour have still not been executed in full.
The relevant provisions of the domestic law and practice are cited in the judgment of Romashov v. Ukraine (no. 67534/01, §§ 16-19, 27 July 2004).
