The case originated in an application (no. 13323/02) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Hungarian national, Mr Antal Majercsik (“the applicant”), on 10 October 2001.
The Hungarian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mr L. Höltzl, Deputy State-Secretary, Ministry of Justice.
On 12 January 2005 the Court decided to communicate the application. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
The applicant was born in 1949 and lives in .
In proceedings instituted by the applicant, on 12 November 1993 the Supreme Court finally declared that his former employer had terminated his employment unlawfully and granted him severance pay and damages. Simultaneously, on 25 May 1993 the applicant brought an action before the  against the employer claiming pecuniary and non-pecuniary damages.
On 15 June 1995 the  dismissed the applicant’s claims. On appeal, on 26 June 1996 the  granted the applicant damages plus accrued interest.
On 15 April 1997 the Supreme Court’s review bench quashed the second-instance decision in its part concerning the pecuniary damages, and remitted this aspect of the case to the .
In the resumed second-instance proceedings, on 29 September 1998 the  granted damages plus accrued interest to the applicant. This decision was partially quashed by the Supreme Court’s review bench on 8 July 1999 for procedural shortcomings.
In the resumed second-instance proceedings, on 29 February 2000 the  granted some damages plus accrued interest to the applicant and dismissed the remainder of his action. On 29 March 2001 the Supreme Court’s review bench partially quashed this decision as the  had miscalculated the amount granted to the applicant.
In the proceedings resumed for the third time, on 30 October 2001 the  granted the applicant damages plus accrued interest.
