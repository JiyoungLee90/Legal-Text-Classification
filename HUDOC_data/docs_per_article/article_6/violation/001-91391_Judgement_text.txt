The case originated in an application (no. 20728/04) against  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Ukrainian-based cooperative association “Kooperativ Kakhovskiy no. 5”.
The Ukrainian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mr Y. Zaytsev.
The applicant association alleged, in particular, violations of Articles 6 § 1 and 13 of the Convention and Article 1 of Protocol No. 1 on account of non-enforcement of a final judgment given in its favour against a public entity.
On 5 December 2005 the Court decided to give notice of the application to the Government. It was also decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility (Article 29 § 3).
The applicant association was registered in 1990 and is located in the town of Kakhovka,  region.
The relevant domestic law is set out in the judgment of 27 July 2004 in the case of Romashov v. Ukraine (no. 67534/01, §§ 16-19).
