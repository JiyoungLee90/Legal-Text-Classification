The case originated in three applications (nos. 24377/15, 59025/16 and 61429/16) against the Russian Federation lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by three Russian nationals. Their details appear in Appendix I below.
Mr Sokolov (application no. 61429/16) was represented by Ms I. Biryukova, a lawyer practising in Podolsk, Moscow Region. The Russian Government (“the Government”) were represented by Mr M. Galperin, Representative of the Russian Federation to the European Court of Human Rights.
On 8 February 2018 notice of the complaints concerning alleged impartiality of the tribunal and courts’ failure to examine witnesses was given to the Government and the remainder of the applications was declared inadmissible pursuant to Rule 54 § 3 of the Rules of Court.
The applicants were found liable for administrative offences. The courts heard the matter in the absence of a prosecuting party. The details pertaining to each application are summed up in Appendix II below.
For a summary of the relevant domestic provisions and practice, see the case of Karelin v. Russia (no. 926/08, §§ 22-37, 20 September 2016).
