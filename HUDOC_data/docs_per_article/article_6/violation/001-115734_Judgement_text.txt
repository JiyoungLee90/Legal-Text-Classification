The case originated in an application (no. 24162/06) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Slovenian company, Kovinar d.o.o. (“the applicant company”), on 22 May 2006.
The Slovenian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent.
On 8 July 2010 the application was communicated to the Government. In accordance with Protocol No. 14, the application was assigned to a committee of three Judges.
The applicant was born in 1949 and lives in Ptuj.
In the beginning of the 1990s the applicant company entered a business relationship with the company JEKLOTEHNA D.D, which was a company owned by the State. In 1996 bankruptcy proceedings were instituted against the latter.
On 16 September 1997 the applicant company instituted proceedings before the Ljubljana District Court against the  seeking the payment of the debt owed by JEKLOTEHNA D.D.
On 14 July 1999 the court held a hearing.
On 6 October 1999 the Ljubljana District Court issued a judgment rejecting the applicant company’s request. The applicant company appealed.
On 17 December 2002 the Ljubljana District Court issued a decision stating that the appeal was considered to be withdrawn, since the applicant company failed to pay the court fees for the appeal. The applicant company appealed.
On 9 June 2004 the  upheld the appeal and annulled the decision. Subsequently, the appeal against the first-instance judgment was allowed.
On 15 June 2005 the  rejected the applicant company’s appeal. The applicant company lodged an appeal on points of law.
On 2 December 2005 the Ljubljana District Court rejected the appeal on points of law on procedural grounds. The applicant company appealed.
On 11 January 2006 the  upheld the appeal. Subsequently the appeal on points of law was accepted and sent to the Supreme Court.
On 9 October 2007 the Supreme Court rejected the appeal on points of law.
For relevant domestic law see Tomažič v. Slovenia (no. 38350/02,
13 December 2007).
