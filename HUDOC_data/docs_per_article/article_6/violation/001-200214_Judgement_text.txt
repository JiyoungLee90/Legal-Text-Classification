The case originated in three applications against Ukraine lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”).
The applicants’ details are set out in the appended tables.
Notice of the complaints raised by the applicants concerning their alleged ill-treatment and the ineffective investigation of their respective allegations was given to the Ukrainian Government together with notice of several other complaints raised by the applicants under the Court’s wellestablished case-law (see appended tables).
The remainder of applications nos. 54012/07 and 25697/08 was declared inadmissible pursuant to Rule 54 § 3 of the Rules of Court.
The Government did not object to the examination of the applications by a Committee.
The applicants complained that they had been subjected to police illtreatment and that the State authorities had failed to carry out an effective investigation into their respective allegations.
They also raised other complaints under the provisions of the Convention subject to the Court’s well-established case-law.
Relevant details are set out in the appended tables.
