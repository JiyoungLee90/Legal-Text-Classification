The case originated in an application (no. 39073/02) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Hungarian national, Mrs Diána Vondratsek (“the applicant”), on 4 October 2002.
The Hungarian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mr L. Höltzl, Deputy State-Secretary, Ministry of Justice.
On 11 May 2005 the Court decided to communicate the application. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
The applicant was born in 1945 and lives in .
On 27 January 1986 the applicant instituted proceedings against her ex-husband for the division of their matrimonial property in the Buda Central District Court.
The District Court held eleven hearings and appointed four experts, and, on 1 March 1990, it gave judgment. On appeal, the  held two hearings and heard another expert. On 1 July 1992 it quashed the first-instance decision and remitted the case.
In the resumed proceedings, the District Court held six hearings between 18 May 1993 and 27 October 1995. At the hearing on 30 January 1996, it appointed a valuation expert who presented his opinion on 26 August 1996. The opinion was completed on 3 December 1996.
In the period between 25 April 1996 and 20 May 1997, four further hearings took place. On 22 July 1997 another expert opinion was obtained.
Between 18 September 1997 and 15 April 1999, the court held ten hearings. On that date it gave judgment, ordering that the common ownership of certain real property be terminated by way of an auction, and granting the applicant the right to buy out another property.
On appeal, on 17 March 2000 the  changed the first-instance decision, transferring the right to buy out to the respondent.
On 2 June 2000 the applicant filed a petition for review which was completed on 6 December 2000.
On 15 April 2002 the Supreme Court upheld the second-instance decision.
