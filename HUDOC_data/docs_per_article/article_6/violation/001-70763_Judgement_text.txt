The case originated in an application (no. 39327/02) against the Republic of Turkey lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Turkish national, Mr İbrahim Bülent Mete, on 26 September 2002.
The applicant was represented by Mr Yılmaz Alkım, a lawyer practising in Kırklareli. The Turkish Government (“the Government”) did not designate an Agent for the purposes of the proceedings before the Court.
On 17 June 2004 the Court declared the application partly inadmissible and decided to communicate the complaint concerning the length of the proceedings. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
On 1 November 2004 the Court changed the composition of its Sections (Rule 25 § 1). This case was assigned to the newly composed Second Section (Rule 52 § 1).
The applicant and the Government each filed observations on the merits (Rule 59 § 1).
The applicant was born in 1961 and lives in .
On 14 May 1990 the Bakirköy District Governorship granted the applicant a licence to serve alcohol in his restaurant.
On an unspecified date, the quarter of Güngören, where the applicant’s restaurant was located, became a separate district. On 13 October 1997, the Güngören District Governorship revoked the applicant’s licence on the ground that the applicant’s restaurant was outside the designated area for restaurants serving alcohol.
On 27 October 1997 the applicant was notified of this decision and was barred from running the restaurant.
On 3 November 1997 the applicant brought an action before the  for the annulment of the Güngören District Governorship’s decision of 13 October 1997. He also requested that the decision be stayed.
On 23 January 1998 the  rejected the applicant’s request for a stay of proceedings. The applicant objected to this decision. On 18 March 1998 the  rejected the applicant’s challenge.
On 11 June 1998 the  delivered its judgment on the merits of the case. The court decided that the revocation order of the Güngören District Governorship was in conformity with the law. The applicant appealed against this judgment to the , and as a preliminary step, he once again requested the  to stay the execution of the Governorship’s decision. On 12 November 1998 the  decided on the request for a stay and rejected it.
On 15 February 2001 the  dismissed the applicant’s appeal on points of law, and upheld the judgment of the first-instance court.
The applicant requested rectification of this decision. On 24 June 2002 the  dismissed the applicant’s request.
On 15 August 2002 the applicant was notified of the ’s final decision.
