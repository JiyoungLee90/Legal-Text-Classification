The case originated in an application (no. 9253/06) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Russian national, Mr Yuriy Vladimirovich Mizyuk (“the applicant”), on 28 January 2006.
The Russian Government (“the Government”) were represented by Mr P. Laptev, the Representaitve of the  at the European Court of Human Rights.
On 4 April 2006 the Court decided to give notice of the application to the Government. Under the provisions of Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility.
The applicant was born in 1953 and lives in the town of .
On 20 July 2004 the Tsentralniy District Court of Omsk accepted the applicant's action against the Omsk Town Council and ordered that the Council should provide the applicant and his family members with a well-equipped flat, taking into account that the applicant had the right to an additional room. The judgment was upheld on appeal by the  on 25 August 2004.
On 16 September 2004 enforcement proceedings were instituted.
On 28 December 2004 the Tsentralniy District Court clarified its judgment of 20 July 2004, noting that the Council should buy a flat at the expense of the federal budget.
The judgment remains unenforced because the Federal Treasury did not provide the Council with necessary funds.
