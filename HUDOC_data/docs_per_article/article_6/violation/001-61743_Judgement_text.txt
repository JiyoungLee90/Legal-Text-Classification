The case originated in an application (no. 68930/01) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Polish national, Ms Joanna Politikin (“the applicant”), on 4 July 2000.
The Polish Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agents, Mr K. Drzewicki and, subsequently, by Ms S. Jaczewska, of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.
On 11 February 2003 the Court decided to communicate the complaint concerning the length of the proceedings to the Government. Under the provisions of Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility.
The applicant was born in 1942 and lives in .
On 21 March 1994 the Municipal Housing Board for WarsawŻoliborz (Zarząd Budynków Komunalnych ”Żoliborz”) lodged with the Warsaw District Court (Sąd Rejonowy) a civil action against the applicant. The plaintiff sought to terminate the payment of an annuity (renta wyrównawcza) to the applicant.
The trial court held hearings on 7 July, 19 October, 12 December 1994 and 9 February 1995.
On 20 February 1995 the District Court exempted the applicant from the court-fees.
On 5 March 1996 the court held a hearing.
On 23 December 1997 the trial court, sitting in camera, ordered an expert opinion. The opinion was submitted to the court in June 1998.
On 9 March 1999 the court held a hearing.
On 7 July 2000 the trial court ordered a supplementary expert opinion.
On 17 July 2001 the trial court decided to exempt the applicant from the costs of the expert opinion.
On 20 August 2002 the expert submitted his opinion to the court.
On 23 October 2002 the court held a hearing.
On 19 November 2002 the Warsaw District Court gave judgment in which it dismissed the plaintiff's claim and increased the applicant's annuity.
The parties did not appeal against it and the judgment became final.
