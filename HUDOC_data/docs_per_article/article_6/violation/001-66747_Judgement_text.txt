The case originated in an application (no. 6735/03) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Polish national, Ms Danuta Durasik (“the applicant”), on 6 February 2003.
The Polish Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mr J. Wołąsiewicz of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.
On 18 February 2004 the President of the Fourth Section decided to communicate the complaint concerning the length of the proceedings to the Government. Under the provisions of Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it was decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility.
The applicant was born in 1951 and lives in Łódź, .
On 4 November 1993 the applicant lodged with the Łódź  (Sąd Wojewódzki) a civil action against the State Treasury – the . She claimed compensation and a monthly pension for damage she had sustained as a result of a medical treatment at the .
On 5 November 1993 the court exempted the applicant from the courtfees.
On 20 March 1995 the trial court held the first hearing.
Subsequently, hearings were held on 12 June and 13 November 1995.
On 27 November 1995 the Łódź  gave judgment. It dismissed the applicant's action. The trial court found no fault on the part of the doctors concerned and accordingly dismissed the liability of the State Treasury for the damage.
The applicant appealed against this judgment.
On 28 February 1996 the Lodz Court of Appeal (Sąd Apelacyjny) quashed the impugned judgment and remitted the case to the .
The trial court held the first hearing on 2 October 1996.
At the subsequent hearings which were held on 29 August and 12 November 1997 the trial court ordered an expert opinion and heard an expert witness.
At the hearing held on 23 January 1998 the defendant asked the court to prepare another expert opinion.
Between 24 January 1998 and 18 January 1999 no hearings were held.
Subsequently, the Łódź  held six hearings and on 22 July 1999 it gave judgment. The trial court again dismissed the action.
The applicant appealed against the judgment.
The Łódź Court of Appeal held five hearings and on 9 October 2001 it gave judgment. The appellate court partly allowed the appeal and awarded the applicant PLN 4,000 in compensation. The court found, inter alia, that the doctors had failed to act diligently; consequently, the State Treasury was liable for the damage sustained by the applicant.
On 29 January 2002 the applicant lodged a cassation appeal with the Supreme Court.
On 29 October 2002 the Supreme Court (Sąd Najwyższy) rejected her cassation appeal.
