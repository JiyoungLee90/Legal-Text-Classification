The case originated in an application (no. 9871/05) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Turkish national, Mr Akın Şahin (“the applicant”), on 28 December 2004. The applicant was represented by Mr M. An, a lawyer practising in . The Turkish Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent.
On 13 May 2009 the President of the Second Section decided to give notice of the application to the Government. It was also decided to examine the merits of the application at the same time as its admissibility (Article 29 § 1).
The applicant, a former lieutenant, was born in 1975 and lives in .
After being expelled from the armed forces for disciplinary and ethical reasons, mainly on account of his excessive indebtedness and his failure to discharge his debts, the applicant brought an action before the  against the Ministry of Defence for the annulment of the expulsion order.
An action for fraud was subsequently filed against the applicant before the criminal courts.
The Ministry of Defence submitted certain documents and information to the  regarding the applicant’s expulsion, which were classified as “secret documents” under Article 52 (4) of Law no. 1602 on the . These documents were not disclosed to the applicant.
On 11 November 2003 the  held a hearing, which the applicant attended, and on 30 March 2004 it rejected the applicant’s case. The court refused to hear the applicant’s witnesses, holding that the witness statements would not make any difference to the applicant’s position in the circumstances of the present case and that the examination could be sufficiently conducted on the basis of the documents in the case file.
On 6 July 2004 the  dismissed the applicant’s rectification request.
A description of the relevant domestic law can be found in the decision of Karayiğit v. Turkey ((dec.), no. 45874/05, 23 September 2008).
