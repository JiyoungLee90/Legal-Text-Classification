The case originated in an application (no. 37294/02) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Slovenian national, Mr Fahir Mušič (“the applicant”), on 10 October 2002.
The applicant was represented by the Verstovšek lawyers. The Slovenian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mr L. Bembič, State Attorney-General.
The applicant alleged under Article 6 § 1 of the Convention that the length of the proceedings before the domestic courts to which he was a party was excessive. In substance, he also complained about the lack of an effective domestic remedy in respect of the excessive length of the proceedings (Article 13 of the Convention).
On 15 September 2004 the Court decided to communicate the complaints concerning the length of the proceedings and the lack of remedies in that respect to the Government. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
The applicant was born in 1956 and lives in Velenje.
On 25 March 1997 the applicant was injured in an accident at work cleaning taps in the bathroom. The applicant’s employer had taken out insurance with the insurance company ZT.
On 6 July 1998 the applicant instituted civil proceedings against ZT in the Celje District Court (Okrožno sodišče v Celju) seeking damages in the amount of 583,330 tolars (approximately 2,430 euros) for the injuries sustained.
Between 2 March 1999 and 6 October 1999 the applicant made three requests that a date be set for a hearing.
Between 7 February 2000 and 28 May 2003 he lodged six preliminary written submissions and/or adduced evidence.
Of the five hearings held between 24 January 2000 and 12 June 2003 one was adjourned at the request of the applicant due to the illness of his legal representatives.
During the proceedings the court appointed a medical expert and work safety expert.
At the last hearing the court decided to deliver a written judgment. The judgment, upholding the applicant’s claim in part, was served on the applicant on 24 June 2003.
On 27 June 2003 the applicant appealed to the  (Višje sodišče v Celju). ZT cross-appealed.
On 5 January 2005 the court allowed the applicant’s appeal in part
The judgment was served on the applicant on 20 January 2005.
