The case originated in two applications (nos. 18820/17 and 20413/17) against the Russian Federation lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by two Russian nationals Mr Sergey Vasilyevich Kadadov and Mr Georgiy Gennadyevich Pereverzev on 22 February and 1 March 2017 respectively.
The Russian Government (“the Government”) were represented by Mr M. Galperin, Representative of the Russian Federation to the European Court of Human Rights.
On 8 February 2018 notice of the complaints concerning alleged partiality of the tribunal were given to the Government and the remainder of the applications was declared inadmissible pursuant to Rule 54 § 3 of the Rules of Court.
The applicants were born in 1976 and 1973 respectively and live in Volsk, Saratov Region.
The applicants were found liable for an administrative offence of drunk driving. The court imposed on them a monetary fine in the amount of 30,000 Russian roubles and suspended temporarily their driving licenses. The details pertaining to each application are summed up in Appendix II below.
For a summary of the relevant domestic provisions and practice, see the case of Karelin v. Russia (no. 926/08, §§ 22-37, 20 September 2016).
