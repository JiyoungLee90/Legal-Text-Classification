The case originated in an application (no. 17330/02) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Slovenian national, Ms Magda Kotnik (“the applicant”), on 22 April 2002.
The applicant was represented by the Verstovšek lawyers. The Slovenian Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent, Mr L. Bembič, State Attorney-General.
The applicant alleged under Article 6 § 1 of the Convention that the length of the proceedings before the domestic courts to which she was a party was excessive. In substance, she also complained about the lack of an effective domestic remedy in respect of the excessive length of the proceedings (Article 13 of the Convention).
On 11 June 2004 the Court decided to communicate the complaints concerning the length of the proceedings and the lack of remedies in that respect to the Government. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
The applicant was born in 1952 and lives in Zreče.
On 10 August 1997 the applicant’s daughter born in 1980 was injured in a car accident. The perpetrator of the accident had taken out insurance with the insurance company ZT.
On 26 March 1999 the applicant instituted civil proceedings against ZT in the Celje District Court (Okrožno sodišče v Celju) seeking damages in the amount of 3,503,330 tolars (approximately 14,600 euros) for the injuries sustained.
Between 27 September 1999 and 25 March 2002 the applicant made six requests that a date be set for a hearing.
Between 30 June 2000 and 15 April 2000 she lodged four preliminary written submissions.
On 12 April 2002 the court held a hearing and decided to appoint two medical experts.
On 24 April 2002 the applicant and her adversary settled the case outside the court. On that same day the applicant withdraw her claim. The parties waived their right to be served a decision on termination of the court proceedings.
