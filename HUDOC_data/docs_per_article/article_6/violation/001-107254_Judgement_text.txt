The case originated in an application (no. 10443/08) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by two Turkish nationals, Mr Naci Akkuş and Mr Necmi Akkuş (“the applicants”), on 15 January 2008. The applicants were represented by Mr A.F. Demirkan, a lawyer practising in . The Turkish Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent.
On 12 June 2009 the President of the Second Section decided to give notice of the application to the Government. It was also decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time (Article 29 § 1).
The applicants were born in 1977 and 1966 respectively and live in .
In 2004 the General Directorate of Highways seized a plot of land belonging to the applicants without any formal expropriation, for the construction of a highway. The applicants brought an action before the  to obtain compensation for the de facto expropriation of their property. They requested 5,500 Turkish liras (TRY) as compensation from the court and reserved their right to increase this claim in due course.
On 29 December 2005 the  awarded the applicants TRY 5,500 as compensation for the de facto expropriation of their land, as requested, plus interest. The applicants initiated execution proceedings before the Bursa Execution Office to obtain that amount (file no. 2006/2200).
On 29 May 2006 the Court of Cassation upheld the judgment of the first-instance court.
The applicants subsequently brought an additional action (“ek dava”) before the  to obtain further compensation for their land in the light of the expert report obtained during the previous proceedings, which had valued the land at a rate higher than that initially requested by them.
On 2 November 2006 the  awarded the applicants TRY 100,723.25, plus interest. The applicants initiated another set of execution proceedings before the Bursa Execution Office to obtain the amount awarded (file no. 2009/2817).
On 13 March 2007 the Court of Cassation upheld the judgment of the .
On 16 January 2009 the administration paid TRY 9,414.62 to file no. 2006/2200 before the Bursa Execution Office in respect of the debt arising from the initial action.
Subsequently, on 24 March 2009 they paid TRY 152,951.97 to file no. 2009/2817 before the Bursa Execution Office in respect of the debt under the additional action. According to the information provided by the applicants, there has been no outstanding debt in the execution files.
