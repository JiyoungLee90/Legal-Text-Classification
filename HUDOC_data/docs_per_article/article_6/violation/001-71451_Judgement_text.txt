The case originated in an application (no. 33914/02) against the  lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by a Russian national, Ms Olga Leonidovna Skorobogatova (“the applicant”), on 16 August 2002.
The Russian Government (“the Government”) were represented by Mr P. Laptev, Representative of the  at the European Court of Human Rights.
On 2 September 2004 the Court decided to communicate the application. Applying Article 29 § 3 of the Convention, it decided to rule on the admissibility and merits of the application at the same time.
The applicant was born in 1956 and lives in Luchegorsk.
In September 1995 the applicant lodged an action before the Severo-Evenkiyskiy District Court of the Magadan Region against the head of the local administration, a transport company and a private individual for compensation for damage.
The Severo-Evenkiyskiy District Court disallowed the action because it should have been lodged before the  which had territorial jurisdiction over it.
On 1 November 1995 the applicant lodged the action before the . She asked the court to examine her claim in her absence, as she lived in .
As the applicant’s claim had not been examined from 1995 to 1998, she complained to various officials about the court’s inactivity.
On 16 August 1998 the Deputy President of the  informed the applicant that it was impossible to expedite the proceedings because the judges were overburdened with cases.
On 4 March 1999 the applicant increased her claim.
A hearing was fixed for 8 April 1999 and adjourned due to the defendants’ absence.
On 28 July 1999 the  dismissed the applicant’s action.
On 24 August 1999 a copy of the judgment was sent to the applicant.
On 14 October 1999 the applicant appealed against the judgment of 28 July 1999.
On 1 November 1999 the  stayed the appeal proceedings and requested the applicant to submit by 30 November 1999 three copies of her statement of appeal and to pay RUR 5 in a court fee.
On 30 November 1999 the judgment of 28 July 1999 became final because the applicant had not paid the fee.
On 16 December 1999 the  returned the statement of appeal.
On 7 June 2000 the Presidium of the , by way of supervisory review, quashed the judgment of 28 July 1999 and remitted the case for a new examination.
On 20 June 2000 the case-file was sent to the town court.
On 10 July 2000 the judge M. was assigned to the case.
On 13 September 2000 the case was transferred to the judge D.
Of six hearings listed between 19 October 2000 and 27 February 2001, four hearings were adjourned. Two hearings were adjourned due to the applicant’s absence. The other hearings were adjourned because the parties did not attend, although the applicant repeatedly asked the court to hold hearings in her absence.
In February 2001 the applicant moved from  to the Primorye Region. She submits that promptly after the move she advised by post the Magadan Town Court of her new address.
On 10 April 2001 the  requested the applicant to submit additional documents. It appears that the instructions were successfully fulfilled by the applicant.
On 14 June 2001 the , in the applicant’s absence, dismissed her action.
On 17 July 2001 the registry of the  sent a copy of the judgment to the applicant’s old address in .
On 14 March 2002 the applicant complained to the  about the town court’s inactivity in her case.
On 2 April 2002 the President of the  replied by a letter that the case had been decided on 14 June 2001. The President also acknowledged that, although the applicant had advised the court of her new address, on 17 July 2001 a copy of the judgment had been mistakenly sent to the applicant’s previous address.
On 1 April 2002 the registry of the town court sent a copy of the judgment of 14 June 2001 to the applicant’s address in .
On 11 September 2002 the President of the  informed the applicant that a copy of the 14 June 2001 judgment had been twice sent to the applicant’s address in  and that the  was told to send a copy of the judgment to her current address in Primorye.
On 12 September 2002 the  sent a copy of the judgment of 14 June 2001 to the applicant’s address in Primorye. She received it on 18 September 2002.
On 3 October 2002 the applicant requested the  to extend the time-limit for lodging an appeal against the judgment of 14 June 2001. The request enclosed a statement of appeal.
On 21 October 2001 the  extended the time-limit and accepted the applicant’s statement of appeal.
On 5 November 2002 the , in the applicant’s absence, upheld the judgment of 14 June 2001.
On 16 December 2002 a copy of the judgment of 5 November 2002 was sent to the applicant.
