The case originated in an application (no. 1236/09) against the Republic of Turkey lodged with the Court under Article 34 of the Convention for the Protection of Human Rights and Fundamental Freedoms (“the Convention”) by four Turkish nationals, Mr Eyyup Yaşar, Mr Cahit Tamur, Mr Fuat Bor and Mr Hüseyin Duman (“the applicants”), on 1 August 2008.
The applicants were represented by Mr S. Kaya, a lawyer practising in Ankara. The Turkish Government (“the Government”) were represented by their Agent.
On 29 September 2010 the application was communicated to the Government.
On 7 October 2016 the Vice-President of the Second Section invited the Government to submit further observations, if they so wished, following the judgment in Ibrahim and Others v. the United Kingdom ([GC], nos. 50541/08 and 3 others, ECHR 2016).
A list of the applicants is set out in the appendix.
On 21 November 1994 the applicants were taken into police custody. Their statements were taken by the police in the absence of a lawyer. All the applicants confessed to having committed the crimes with which they had been charged. On 28 December 1994 they were remanded in custody.
On 24 January 1995 the Public Prosecutor at the Diyarbakır State Security Court filed a bill of indictment against the applicants (except for Eyyup Yaşar), charging them under Section 125 of the former Criminal Code with carrying out activities with the aim of bringing about the secession of part of the national territory.
On 18 June 1999 the Constitution was amended and the military judges sitting on the bench of the State Security Courts were replaced by civilian judges.
On 4 December 2001 the  convicted the applicants as charged.
On 20 November 2002 the Court of Cassation quashed the convictions.
State Security Courts were abolished on 16 June 2004 pursuant to Law no. 5190. The case was accordingly transferred to the .
On 22 February 2007 the  again convicted the applicants under Section 125 of the Criminal Code and sentenced them to life imprisonment.
On 6 February 2008 the Court of Cassation upheld the convictions.
A description of the relevant domestic law concerning the right of access to a lawyer may be found in Salduz v. Turkey ([GC] no. 36391/02, §§ 2731, ECHR 2008).
On 15 July 2003 Law no. 4928 repealed Section 31 of Law no. 3842, thus the restriction on an accused’s right of access to a lawyer in proceedings before the State Security Courts was lifted.
