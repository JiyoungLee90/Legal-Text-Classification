The applicant was born in 1968 and lives in Ankara. He was represented by Ms S. Karaduman, a lawyer practising in Ankara.
The Government were represented by their Agent.
The facts of the case, as submitted by the parties, may be summarised as follows.
In 2007, criminal proceedings were initiated against the applicant for having referred to Abdullah Öcalan as “Sayın”, meaning esteemed, in a press statement.
On 24 December 2009, in a final judgment, the Diyarbakır Magistrates’ Court convicted the applicant as charged under Article 215 of the Criminal Code and sentenced him to twenty five days’ imprisonment. In accordance with Article 50 of the Criminal Code, the applicant’s prison sentence was converted into a fine.
A full description of the relevant domestic law may be found in Yalçınkaya and others v. Turkey (nos. 25764/09 and 18 others, §§ 1213, 1 October 2013).
